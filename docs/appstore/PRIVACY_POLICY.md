# Privacy Policy — Universal Remote (LG & Vizio)

**Effective date: June 13, 2026**

Thank you for using Universal Remote ("the app"). Your privacy matters. This
policy explains, in plain language, what the app does and does not do with your
information.

## The short version

**The app collects no personal data. It has no user accounts, no analytics, no
advertising, and no third-party trackers.** Everything the app does happens on
your own device and your local Wi-Fi network.

## What the app accesses, and why

- **Local Network access.** The app asks for permission to access devices on
  your local network. This is required to discover your TV and send it remote-
  control commands over Wi-Fi. The app communicates **only** with TVs on your own
  network — it does not send this information anywhere else.
- **Pairing credentials.** When you pair with a TV, the TV may return a token (for
  example, an LG client-key or a Vizio auth token) so you don't have to re-approve
  the app every time. This token, along with the TV's name and network address, is
  stored **only on your device** (in the app's local storage) so it can reconnect
  later. It is never transmitted to us or any third party.

## What the app does NOT do

- It does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information about you.
- It does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.
- It does not require or offer an account or login.
- It does not send any data to the developer or to any external server.
- It does not access your contacts, photos, location, microphone, or camera.

## Data sharing

The app shares no data with anyone, because it collects no data to share.

## Children's privacy

The app does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children
under 13.
